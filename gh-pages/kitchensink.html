P

<p>The standard paragraph font. Set at a base size of 1rem, or 16 pixels usually. Keep paragraphs limited to 2 or 3 sentences, maybe 4 at most to avoid wall-of-text issues.</p>
<p>Paragraphs are limited to 60rem width, maximum, to alieviate reading fatigue at large screen sizes. Centered as a block, text-aligned left.</p>

The standard paragraph font. Set at a base size of 1rem, or 16 pixels usually. Keep paragraphs limited to 2 or 3 sentences, maybe 4 at most to avoid wall-of-text issues.

Paragraphs are limited to 60rem width, maximum, to alieviate reading fatigue at large screen sizes. Centered as a block, text-aligned left.

<ul>

	<li>Each list item is prefeixed denoted by a line icon (the "list-style-type" parameter in CSS).</li>
	<li>At the <strong>top level</strong> a <strong>small, filled disc</strong>.</li>
	<li>Nesting a list... 
		<ul>
			<li>...is a useful thing, sometimes.</li>
			<li>The <strong>second level</strong> line icon is a <strong>stroked disc</strong>.</li>
		</ul>
	</li>
	<li>
		In exceptional cases
		<ul>
			<li> People nest.....
				<ul>
					<li>...Inside a nest</li>
					<li>So is that nest squared? Or cubed?</li>
					<li>The <strong>third level</strong> is denoted by a <strong>filled square</strong>. No-one has ever ventured deeper.</li>
				</ul>
			</li>
		</ul>
	</li>
<ul>
  • This is the first list item.
  • Each list item is prefeixed denoted by a line icon (the "list-style-type" parameter in CSS).
  • At the top level a small, filled disc.
  • Nesting a list...
    • ...is a useful thing, sometimes.
    • The second level line icon is a stroked disc.
  • In exceptional cases
    • People nest.....
      • ...Inside a nest
      • So is that nest squared? Or cubed?
      • The third level is denoted by a filled square. No-one has ever ventured deeper.

Ordered List

<ol>
	<li>This is the first list item</li>
	<li>Ordered lists are <strong>numbered</strong> at the <strong>top level</strong></li>
	<li>When nesting a list inside a list... 
		<ol>
			<li>...the <strong>second level</strong> is prefixed by a <strong>lower alpha character</strong></li>
		</ol>
	</li>
	<li>
		In exceptional cases...
		<ol>
			<li>...deeply nested lists, like three (3) levels deep...
				<ol>
					<li>...the item is prefixed by <strong>roman numerals</strong></strong>...</li>
					<li>...to maintain the differentiation.</li>
				</ol>
			</li>
		</ol>
	</li>
<ol>
  1. This is the first list item
  2. Ordered lists are numbered at the top level
  3. When nesting a list inside a list...
    1. ...the second level is prefixed by a lower alpha character
  4. In exceptional cases...
    1. ...deeply nested lists, like three (3) levels deep...
      1. ...the item is prefixed by roman numerals...
      2. ...to maintain the differentiation.
